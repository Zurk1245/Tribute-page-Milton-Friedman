Milton Friedman:
Here we have a time line with the most important events of his life:
  • 1912: Born in New York.
  • 1932: Graduated from Rutgers University with a specialization in mathematics and economics.
  • 1943: Joined the Division of War Research at Columbia University, where he spent the rest of the World War II working as a mathematical statistician.
  • 1946: Accepted an offer to work in the Chicago University to teach economic theory.
  • 1976: Recieves the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences for his research on consumption analysis, monetary history and theory and the complexity of stabilization policy.
  • 1988: Recieves the National Medal of Science and the Presidential Medal of Freedom.
  • 2006: Dies of heart failure at the age of 94 in San Francisco.
